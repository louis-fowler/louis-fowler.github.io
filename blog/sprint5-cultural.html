Reflection - Foundations Core Skills

12/5/20

What are three new things you have learned about yourself and your ego as a result of the core learning?

That I don't to enforce work hours for programming because I enjoy it so much. I would constantly look forward to 'getting to work' the next day.I'm very good at loosing myself down rabbit holes, I will spend hours trying to sort something small out if I know I can. That leads to the third thing - I find it very hard to admit defeat, if I get lost and I've used up all available learning avenues (google, asking for help) I still cant move on until its perfect.

What are the role of values, empathy and self awareness in learning and programming?

Empathy and self awareness are very important, as shown in the last point in the previous question. If I had better self awareness and empathy towards myself I could move on. Often moving on and coming back later works things out!

What has surprised you the most about the core learning?

How much of it I was already aware of - Mindfulness has been a core part of my life for a long time!

What were the most challenging aspects of the core learning?

I guess the looping things. Talking to a stranger (soon to be friend) for two minutes straight was weird. Listening was even weirder!

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development bootcamp course?

Its a part of the learning process. If you want to work in the programming industry theres more you need to learn than just programming.

Does the time you spent studying core learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practicing programming instead? Justify your answer.

No, I understand where yall are coming from. Nowadays the computer dev isn't some person locked in a room, only allowed out once they've finished their job. YOu have to know how to interact with others if you want to be successful in this industry

Reflect on your learning

What is your long term goal and/or career pathway?

My long term goal will be to find a career in the programming industry and to work my way up from there. I would hope to work in an environment where I can learn but also do my own thing. I have always thought I would prefer working in a workplace setting but this lockdown has got me enjoying programming from home again - with no distractions!

hat do you think your biggest strengths and limitations will be in Bootcamp?

I think my biggest strength is my passion for learning and helping others where I can. I feel that asking questions is important and trying to answer them even if you're not to sure. I've realised over the foundation period that one of my biggest limitations is not being able to let things go. I will get something working great but then spend 10 more hours making it perfect when it doesn't need to be. Get it working well, move on, come back if need be

What do you think your biggest non-technical challenge at Bootcamp will be?

I think that showing people my work will be the hardest part. spending hours/days on something then showing someone my work for critiquing will be nerve racking. What if its not good enough? what if its bug filled etc etc. what if it isn't PERFECT. Even work that I've worked on with others. What will others think of it...

What non-technical skills - human skills - would you like to see developed in yourself while at EDA?

I think working on my feelings towards the answer above. Work on giving and receiving feedback. I also think developing leadership skills is important, as freelance work in teh industry can be quite lucrative. To be good at it you have to have drive and ability to work with clients one on one. Those skills would be important to learn.

What are your expectations of yourself on Bootcamp

Be collaborative - make sure that the alone time programming is mixed up with group programming. Be social. Make sure to realise the context of the environment. It will be stressful. Manage that stress. Be excited to ask for help, I will be surrounded by people who know things I don't, so use it!
