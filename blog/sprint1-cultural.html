Identity, Values and Strengths

22/4/20

Explain a situation where you have had an ethical decision to make.

Reporting bullying in the workplace. It was a pretty difficult decision to make. On the one hand it was completely unacceptable to way I was being treated, nobody should have to put up with that sort of shit. On the other hand I don't want to get anyone fired and I don't want to look like a nark and be removed from the colleges friend group. Also, the person who was being an asshole was a close friend before. This person has a bad temper and cannot handle stress. They also have brain trauma. The way they acted was still unacceptable and they had to be reported. They were given a warning and I avoided that person for a while. I was apologised too. After a while I forgave them and was able to rekindle our friendship. They have not talked to me like that again. I believe they learned from their mistake.

Describe how your culture has influenced your values and identity.

I've been very lucky to grow up in the surroundings I did. Always been surrounded by family. Both sides have large families and very close with most members. So family is very important to me. My family and friends have always been open and excepting of what I want to do and who I am. I believe that has become a part of who I am as well.

Evaluate your strengths and limitations in terms of your learning and career development

I think my biggest strength is my love of learning. I have already seen it take over in this first week of the foundations course. Every new piece of information was exciting to hear. Even the stuff I already knew, being explained in a different way was exciting. It will be a perfect fit for the flipped learning classroom. I constantly find myself being distracted from my video games/stories by googling a solution to a code problem I have, or asking a friend for advice. Limitations that worry me at the moment are mainly that of motivation and focus. At the moment everything is fine however I know how I can get working from home, especially when I get stuck on something. (pomodoro has already worked really well for that (if i remember to set that bloody timer!))

Identify which of your strengths might help you in your learning journey and how they might intersect with learning obstacles.

As i mentioned I think that my love of learning will really help me with the flipped classroom form of teaching. Being given resources to learn from and expand my skill set is extremely exciting. I think this will def help out with focus. As I mentioned before i'm already being distracted from normal daily things with programming problems because I want to learn how to solve them.

Share an example from your experience of where you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension.

With regards to the first question, I have worked with some pretty toxic people. So a lot of tension. It may not be they healthiest strat but I got to the point where the best option was to just avoid them. I had done all I could to try and work around it and it hadn't worked out, so avoidance was the only option. It was effective in the sense that we were then both able to do our work without the distraction of each other. However it broke the team environment , which was stupid. I would probably do the same thing now. If there was tension that couldn't be sorted, I would talk to my boss and if nothing came of it I would avoid.
