Learning Reflections

22/4/20

What is your long term goal and or career pathway?

Long term career goal is to work full time as dev. Front end is what I enjoy the most at the moment so probably something in that field. As this course goes on I hope i can find other avenues of programing I enjoy. I have done a bit of freelance before and although I did enjoy it working from home got to distracting and constantly looking for my next job got lame pretty quick. So I think a job in an office around other people would be ideal.

What human skills would you like to see developed in yourself while at EDA?

Being able to work in a team is exciting. As I have only even done dev work as a freelancer working in a co-operative environment would be a very nice change

A description of your own strengths and limitations

I love to learn. So when it comes to stuff I don't know i'm always excited about the prospect of teaching myself what I need to know. "I've never heard of css flex and grid before, lets jump down that rabbit hole" This can also be a bit of a limitation though, as often those rabbit hole jumps distract from the work I'm meant to be doing

How will you manage your workload in this programme.

I have to be very strict with myself. Set a day start time and finish time. Only let myself have breaks when i've pre-arranged (pomodoro). I think with the use of tools like slack and github working with others will not be a problem, If anything im excited about the idea :)

How will you seek help?

I generally have no problem asking questions. If you don't know the answer to a question theres no embarrassment in trying to learn. So asking Joe or other facilitators questions is fine with me. I'm also lucky to have supportive friends who work in the industry that are happy to help me

What I expect from the facilitation team?

Support and guidance. I expect to be taught new ideas then supported if i don't understand. I expect to feel comfortable asking questions. I expect to make some new friends :)

Other commitments

Counselling once a week, usually wednesday afternoon
